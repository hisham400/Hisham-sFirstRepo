History of Football

The Early Days of Football

Modern football has ancient roots. The first team games using a ball date back over 3,000 years to Mesoamerican cultures. The Aztecs played a version called Tchatali, where the ball symbolized the sun, and losing team captains were sacrificed.

Ancient Aztec ball game

In China, Cuju was played with a round leather ball filled with fur or feathers during the 3rd‚Äì2nd centuries BC. Japan later developed its version called Kemari. Greeks and Romans had handball versions like episkyros and harpastum.

Japanese Kemari match

In England, football was mentioned as early as 1174. The Normans likely influenced it, and later accounts from 1612 show players using hands, similar to volleyball.

Evolution of Modern Football in Britain

In the 19th century, football developed in British schools like Eton and Rugby. Each school had different rules ‚Äî Rugby favoring running with the ball and Eton focusing on dribbling. These differences caused confusion, which led to standardization.

Old school football at Eton and Rugby

Establishing the Rules and the Football Association

In 1843, Cambridge University attempted to unify football rules. In 1863, London clubs founded the Football Association (FA), which banned handling the ball. By 1870, only goalkeepers could use their hands. These rules shaped modern football worldwide.

Premier League Governance

1. FIFA ‚Äì Global Authority

FIFA, formed in 1904, governs world football. It sets rules, organizes global tournaments like the World Cup, and supervises 211 national associations.

2. UEFA ‚Äì Continental Authority

UEFA is Europe's football authority. It runs tournaments like the UEFA Champions League and works with national bodies like the FA in England.

3. The FA ‚Äì National Authority

The FA governs football in England, ensures rules are followed, manages referees, and connects to both FIFA and UEFA for international cooperation.

4. The Premier League ‚Äì League Operator

The Premier League, founded in 1992, is England‚Äôs top-tier league. It runs independently but under FA rules. It handles fixtures, broadcasting, and commercial aspects of the competition.
