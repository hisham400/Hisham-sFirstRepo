A Dive into English Premier League Football

History of Football

The Early Days of Football

Modern football has ancient roots. The first team games using a ball date back over 3,000 years to Mesoamerican cultures. The Aztecs played a version called Tchatali, where the ball symbolized the sun, and losing team captains were sacrificed.

In China, Cuju was played with a round leather ball filled with fur or feathers during the 3rd–2nd centuries BC. Japan later developed its version called Kemari. Greeks and Romans had handball versions like episkyros and harpastum.

In England, football was mentioned as early as 1174. The Normans likely influenced it, and later accounts from 1612 show players using hands, similar to volleyball.

Evolution of Modern Football in Britain

In the 19th century, football developed in British schools like Eton and Rugby. Each school had different rules, with Rugby favoring running and Eton emphasizing dribbling. These differences led to confusion and the need to standardize rules.

Establishing the Rules of the Game and the Formation of the Football Association

In 1843, Cambridge University tried to unify football rules. In 1863, London clubs created the Football Association (FA), banning handling the ball. By 1870, only goalkeepers could handle it. The FA's rules influenced global football and led to cooperation with FIFA.

Governance for Premier League Football

1. FIFA – Global Authority

FIFA, formed in 1904, governs world football. It sets rules, organizes global tournaments like the World Cup, and supervises national associations. Today, FIFA has 211 members and remains football’s top authority.

2. UEFA – Continental Authority

UEFA, the Union of European Football Associations, oversees football in Europe. It manages tournaments like the Champions League and works with national bodies like the FA in England.

3. The FA – National Authority

The FA governs football in England and is linked with both UEFA and FIFA. It creates rules, handles discipline, and ensures English football follows international standards.

4. Premier League – Domestic League Operator

The Premier League is a private company managing top-tier English football. It operates under FA rules but handles its own competition structure and commercial activities.
